Most epoxy failures come down to one thing: the installer skipped proper prep. Acid washing, shot blasting, or just applying product over a dirty floor — these all lead to delamination within months.

We diamond-grind every floor we coat. It takes longer and costs more than the shortcuts, but it's the only prep method that creates the mechanical bond needed for a permanent installation. Our floors don't peel because we don't cut corners where it counts.
